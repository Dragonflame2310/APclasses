In-Depth Guide to AP Courses

AP World History

AP World History explores global patterns of historical development and exchange from c. 1200 CE to the present. Students analyze primary and secondary sources, compare cultures and civilizations, and understand continuity and change over time. Key themes include state-building, economic systems, social structures, and human-environment interaction. The course emphasizes historical reasoning skills like causation, comparison, and contextualization, with writing practice in the form of document-based and long essay questions.

AP Psychology

AP Psychology introduces students to the scientific study of behavior and mental processes. Major topics include biological bases of behavior, sensation and perception, learning and cognition, motivation and emotion, developmental psychology, personality, testing, abnormal behavior, and social psychology. The course also covers research methods and ethical considerations, preparing students to analyze psychological studies and apply psychological concepts in real-world scenarios.

AP Macroeconomics

AP Macroeconomics focuses on the principles that apply to the economy as a whole. Topics include economic indicators (GDP, inflation, unemployment), national income, economic growth, fiscal and monetary policy, and international trade. Students learn how to interpret economic data, use economic models like the Aggregate Demand/Aggregate Supply model, and evaluate policy decisions. The course builds critical thinking about economic systems and real-world economic issues.

AP Biology

AP Biology covers foundational concepts in biology through inquiry-based investigations and lab work. Major units include evolution, cellular processes (including energy and communication), genetics, information transfer, ecology, and interactions. Students analyze data, construct models, and engage in experimental design. The course emphasizes evidence-based reasoning and the application of biological concepts to novel scenarios.

AP Chemistry

AP Chemistry is a college-level course that explores chemical principles and reactions through quantitative and qualitative analysis. Topics include atomic structure, molecular bonding, chemical reactions, thermodynamics, kinetics, equilibrium, and electrochemistry. Lab work is integral, requiring students to design experiments, collect data, and interpret results. The course builds problem-solving skills and reinforces conceptual understanding of chemical processes.
